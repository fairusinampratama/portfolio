## Context and operational problem

ISP billing connects financial records with customer subscriptions, communication, and network access. A billing application therefore needs clear state transitions and safeguards around invoice changes, payments, isolation, reconnection, and router synchronization.

E-Billing was designed to centralize those workflows while also preparing legacy information for a relational data model.

## Deployment and adoption boundary

The application was deployed and technically completed, but it was **not fully adopted as the company's replacement billing platform**. Deployment and technical completion are not presented as complete organizational adoption.

## Workflow and relational design

Customer, subscription, package, invoice, payment, router, and communication records have different lifecycles. The design keeps those concerns related without collapsing them into one status field.

Connection-control actions are tied to explicit customer and payment state. Router synchronization also stages differences for review rather than assuming that a router record and an application record are automatically equivalent.

## Migration and reconciliation

Migration work processed **more than 3,000 source records**. This figure describes source records handled during extraction, cleaning, mapping, and reconciliation. It does not mean 3,000 active customers, 3,000 final clean records, or 3,000 users of the deployed application.

The migration path used scripts to gather legacy information, normalize inconsistent values, map relationships, identify duplicates or conflicts, and prepare reviewed imports.

## Integrations and delivery

The platform covered manual and gateway-oriented payment workflows, generated invoices, customer communication through WhatsApp, MikroTik isolation and reconnection, and router synchronization. Redis-backed queues and deployment configuration supported background processing and production operation.

## Engineering decisions

- Keep invoice, payment, and connection state explicit and auditable.
- Serialize network-enforcement work through dedicated queues.
- Reconcile router-only and application-only records before changing either side.
- Treat legacy migration as a reviewable data pipeline.
- Keep credentials and production data outside the repository.

## What this demonstrates

E-Billing shows Fairus working across financial workflow design, relational data, external network operations, background processing, migration, and deployment while distinguishing technical completion from adoption.
